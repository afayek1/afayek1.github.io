Margins, Borders, and Padding

September 10, 2014

The difference between margins, borders, and paddings can be confusing at first, but here's one way to think about them. Content is typically stored in some sort of container, like a div for example. Now think about this div as a rectangle. A border represents the thickness of the rectangle. At this point, a margin can be thought of as a forcefield around this rectangle, where the space between the thickness of the rectangle and the forcefield is empty. Now, on the inside of this rectangle is padding. Just like you might pack, or better yet, pad contents in a box with styrofoam, think about a rectangle having this same padding, where just like the forcefield, nothing can be in between the padding and the border of the box.
