Stereotype Threat in Tech

October 5, 2014

Stereotype threat is a topic in social psychology that examines the effects a person can endure when they feel they are at risk of conforming to a negative stereotype about their social group. For example, the notion that “girls aren’t good at science” is thought to be a contributor to why women are scarce in science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM). In my case, as a mostly self-taught programmer, I sometimes question whether I have the authority to speak on topics related to computer science. These feelings, through various mechanisms (often anxiety), have ultimately been shown to reduce the performance of an individual.

Can you think of how stereotype threat has affected you? Take a moment to think about it. Think of how it made you feel and think of how you can ensure it doesn’t happen to other people. Need a hint? Maybe you’re the “Americanized” relative or a minority that feels capable or less intelligent than other groups. It can even be something that seems silly like “white people can’t dance.”
